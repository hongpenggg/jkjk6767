Anywayyy, let's move on to a very very very quirky hobby of mine: perfumery. Yes, you read that right, I do indeed sniff, buy, use and make perfumes.

Broadly speaking, perfumes can be divided into several different types depending on their oil concentrations. (Most perfumes are derived from fragrance oils dissolved in an alcohol solvent. Except Muslim/Arabic perfumes, which tend to avoid alcohol solvents due to religious reasons.) From less oils to more oils, fragrances can be divided into:

  • Eau Fraîche(French for 'Fresh Water', 1-3% oils, 1-2hrs of longetivity),
  • Eau de Cologne(French for 'Water of Cologne', 2-4% oils, 1-2hrs of longetivity),
  • Eau de Toilette(French for 'Water of toilet', 5-15% oils, 3-6hrs of longetivity),
  • Eau de Parfum(French for 'Water of perfume', 15-20% oils, 6-10hrs of longetivity),
  • Parfum(French for 'Perfume', 20-40% oils, 10-24hrs of longetivity),
  • Extrait de Parfum(French for 'Perfume Extract', >20% oils, >10hrs of longetivity),

Types of perfumes

Eau Fraîche

The fragrance with the least concentration, usually containing only 1-3% of fragrance oils. Very weak and mild. Extremely short longetivity of 1-2hr. Suited for a quick refresh, hot weather and those whose skin is sensitive to alcohol.

Eau de Cologne

The fragrance with a slightly higher concentration than Eau Fraîche, usually containing 2-4% of fragrance oils. Mild and refreshing. Short longetivity of 1-2hr. Suited for casual use, especially when you don't want an overpowering scent/fragrance. Although the word "Cologne" is commonly used to refer to men's perfumes, eau de colognes refer to any fragrance, regardless of gender, that meets the requirement of 2-4% oil concentration. Additionally, mens' fragrances are not limited to eau de colognes, and instead span a wide range of concentrations, from eau de colognes to extrait de parfums.

Eau de Toilette

Eau de Toilette, often shortened to simply EdT in online fragrance forums, describes a fragrance with a higher oil concentration than Eau de Cologne, usually containing 5-15% of fragrance oils. Fresh and bright, EdTs usually focus on top and heart notes. Moderate longetivity of 3-6hrs. Suited for daily use at home or at the office, especially when you want a subtle scent that lasts longer than an eau fraîche or eau de cologne but does not exterminate your colleagues'/family members' sense of smell.
